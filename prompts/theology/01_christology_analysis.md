# Christology Analysis

## Purpose
Analyze Christological concepts in the Book of Mormon and assess their historical fit.

## Module
Theological Anachronism

## Version
1.0 (2025-01-29)

## Prompt Text

```
Analyze the Christological content of the following Book of Mormon passage.

PASSAGE:
[INSERT PASSAGE]

CLAIMED DATE OF COMPOSITION: [e.g., "c. 550 BC" or "c. 75 BC" or "c. 34 AD"]

TASK: Assess the Christological concepts present and their relationship to the claimed timeframe.

ANALYSIS DIMENSIONS:

1. CHRISTOLOGICAL CONCEPTS PRESENT
   - Names/titles used for Christ
   - Roles attributed to Christ (Messiah, Savior, Redeemer, Creator, etc.)
   - Nature of Christ (divine, human, both)
   - Relationship to Father
   - Pre-existence concepts
   - Atonement mechanism

2. HISTORICAL CONTEXT: PRE-CHRISTIAN JEWISH EXPECTATIONS
   What Messianic/Christological concepts existed in Second Temple Judaism?
   - Davidic Messiah expectations
   - Suffering Servant interpretations
   - Divine intermediary figures
   - Pre-exilic vs. post-exilic developments

3. HISTORICAL CONTEXT: EARLY CHRISTIAN DEVELOPMENT
   How did Christian Christology develop?
   - New Testament diversity
   - Patristic developments
   - Creedal formulations

4. HISTORICAL CONTEXT: 19TH-CENTURY PROTESTANTISM
   What Christological formulations were current in 1820s America?
   - Protestant orthodoxy
   - Restorationist movements
   - Trinitarian vs. anti-Trinitarian debates

OUTPUT FORMAT:

## Christological Analysis: [Passage Reference]

### Concepts Identified
[List of Christological concepts with textual citations]

### Historical Fit Assessment

#### Fit with Claimed Timeframe ([date])
[Analysis of whether these concepts fit what would be expected]
- Concepts that fit: [list with explanation]
- Concepts that seem anachronistic: [list with explanation]
- Concepts that are ambiguous: [list with explanation]

#### Fit with 19th-Century Context
[Analysis of whether these concepts match 19th-century Protestant formulations]
- Concepts that match: [list with explanation]
- Concepts that differ: [list with explanation]
- Concepts that are ambiguous: [list with explanation]

### Hypothesis Implications

**Hypothesis A (Ancient Origin):**
Steelman explanation for any apparently anachronistic concepts:
- Prophetic foreknowledge of Christ
- Divine revelation to pre-Christian prophets
- Diversity of pre-exilic Israelite religion
- [Other principled explanations]

Assessment of steelman strength: [Strong/Moderate/Weak]

**Hypothesis B (19th-Century Origin):**
Steelman explanation for any apparently ancient-fitting concepts:
- Biblical imitation
- Access to scholarly sources
- Coincidental correspondence
- [Other principled explanations]

Assessment of steelman strength: [Strong/Moderate/Weak]

### Overall Assessment
[Which hypothesis does this evidence favor, if either?]

### Confidence Level
[High/Moderate/Low/Indeterminate]

### Key Uncertainties
- Scholarly disagreement about [topic]
- Limited evidence for [area]
- Complexity of [issue]

### Sources to Consult
[Relevant scholarly works for verification]
```

## Expected Output Format
Structured analysis following the template above.

## Notes
- Christology is one of the most contested areas of Book of Mormon analysis
- Be especially careful to steelman both positions
- Note the difference between "concept present in the text" and "anachronism"
- Pre-Christian messianic expectations were more diverse than often assumed
- 19th-century Christology was also diverse across denominations
